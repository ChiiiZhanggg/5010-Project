# Dynamic Delta Hedging Performance Files

Generated by `black_scholes_model.ipynb`, Job C section.

## Main handoff files
- `hedge_performance_series.parquet`
- `hedge_performance_series.csv`
- `report_tables/hedge_summary.csv`
- `report_tables/hedge_rebalance_frequency_summary.csv`

## Simulation convention
The strategy is a short one-option position hedged with `+delta` shares of SPY. The initial option premium funds the hedge. The cash account accrues at the risk-free rate between trading dates. On rebalancing dates, the stock position is adjusted to the current Black-Scholes delta. At expiration, the stock position is liquidated and the option payoff is settled.

## Rebalancing frequencies
{'daily': 1, 'every_2_days': 2, 'weekly': 5}

## Transaction-cost assumptions, in basis points
[0.0, 1.0, 5.0]

## Key series columns
- `target_delta`: Black-Scholes delta used as target stock shares.
- `stock_shares`: post-trade hedge shares.
- `trade_shares`: number of SPY shares bought or sold on that row.
- `cash_account`: cash after accrual, trading, and costs.
- `hedge_asset_value`: cash plus stock value before expiration settlement.
- `mark_to_market_hedging_error`: hedge asset value minus option mark value.
- `cumulative_transaction_cost`: cumulative estimated trading cost.
- `terminal_hedging_pnl`: final replication error after stock liquidation and option payoff settlement.

## Interpretation
A terminal hedging P&L close to zero means the discrete hedge replicated the option payoff well.
Larger positive or negative values indicate hedging error from discrete rebalancing, volatility input choice, realized price movement, and transaction costs.
